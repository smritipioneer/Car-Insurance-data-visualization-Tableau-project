Vehicle Insurance Claims – Tableau Story & Dashboard
📌 Overview
This project analyzes insurance claim data to understand how vehicle type, travel time, and demographics impact claim frequency, claim amount, and risk score.
The Tableau story and dashboards provide a visual narrative that helps identify high-risk segments so the insurance company can manage risk and pricing more effectively.

🛠️ Business Problem
Insurance companies face challenges in balancing profitability with customer risk.
Key questions addressed in this project:

Which car types contribute most to claim frequency and claim amount?

How does travel time vary across vehicle types?

Which driver demographics (occupation, age, education) show higher claim frequency?

What is the relationship between risk score and claim behavior?

📂 Dataset
Total Claims: ~$11.3 million

Key Variables:

Car type (SUV, Minivan, Pickup, Sports Car, Van, Panel Truck)

Claim frequency

Claim amount

Travel time

Risk score

Driver demographics (occupation, age, education, gender, income)

📊 Dashboards & Story Highlights
Claim Frequency by Car Type → SUV drivers have the highest claim frequency (~1831), followed by Minivan drivers.

Claim Amount by Car Type → SUVs and Pickups contribute the largest share of claim amounts.

Travel Time by Car Type → SUVs and Minivans show the longest travel times, indicating higher exposure.

Risk Score by Car Type → SUVs carry the highest risk score (~1,051,799).

Demographic Segmentation → Blue Collar and Clerical occupations show higher claim frequencies.

Education Pareto Analysis → Majority of claim amounts are concentrated among High School and Bachelor’s level drivers.

📈 Key Insights
SUVs and Minivans are high-risk segments due to both high claim frequency and claim amounts.

Travel time correlates with higher claim exposure — longer travel times lead to more claims.

Trailer views vs. content views analogy in OTT projects applies here: risk score strongly predicts claim behavior.

Demographics matter — occupation and education levels show clear patterns in claim frequency.

Weekend drivers and family vehicles tend to have higher claim rates, suggesting lifestyle-driven risk.

💡 Business Recommendations
Adjust premium pricing for SUVs and Minivans to reflect higher risk.

Introduce driver education programs for high-frequency segments (Blue Collar, Clerical).

Use travel time monitoring to identify exposure-heavy drivers.

Focus on risk-based segmentation rather than just claim amount.

Apply Pareto principle — target top segments that contribute ~80% of claim amounts.
